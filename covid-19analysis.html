COVID-19 ANALYSIS

The World Health Organization (WHO) provides an overview of the COVID-19 dataset through its dashboard, which includes data on confirmed cases and deaths, vaccination data, and population data.

Brief Overview

The report focuses on the trends within the 5 continents, i.e., (Africa, South America, North America, Oceania, Asia, Europe), providing a comprehensive overview of the impact of covid 19 across location(s), from 2020 - 2023.

Key Insights

  • There is a constant upward shift in total case and excess mortality from year 2020 to year 2022 then a great decrease in year 2023.
  • There is a strong positive correlation between the total case per million and excess mortality, the more the total case the more the excess mortality.
  • Countries with low poverty rate had more death compared to countries with high poverty rate.
  • Countries with high GDP had more death compared to countries with low GDP.
  • The factors that contributed to fight against Covid-19 are; Stringency Index, Total Vaccinations, Boosters and Gross Domestic Product.
  • Human Development Index was 1% for most countries affected, that is human development index wasn’t a strong combative measure.
  • The factors that contributed to Covid-19 are; Cardiovascular disease, Diabetes, Smoking, Limited Hospital Beds, Gross Domestic Products and Human Development Index.

    • Africa

    Mayotte was greatly affected by the covid 19 pandemic, followed by South Africa in Africa. Mayotte experienced a rapid increase in excess mortality from 0.08% to 0.4% from year 2020 to year 2022 and slightly reduced to 0.2% in 2023. This increase also reflected in the total case from year 2020 (0.007%) to year 2021 (0.06%) and a slight reduction in year 2022 (0.055%) and rapid fall in total case in year 2023 (0.006%). The dataset did not reflect any combative measures available for this country. Mayotte constitutes 0.03% of the Africa’s population, and 0.001% of the total population. South Africa had total population of 60m (approximately), which is 6% of Africa’s population and 0.19% in total population. In South Africa, there was a robust rise in excess mortality from 0.054% in year 2020 to 0.8% in year 2021 and rose continuously to 1.37% in 2022 and greatly decreased to 0.7% in 2023. Total case experienced exponential increase from 0.02% in year 2020 to 0.15% in year 2022. There was no total case of covid 19 in 2023. In year 2020, South Africa had the highest ICU patients and weekly hospital admissions. Covid 19 combative measures such as Stringency Index, Booster, provision of Handwashing Facilities, Gross Domestic Products (GDP), Human Development Index (HDI). Vaccination helped reduce the impact of covid in South Africa. Reproduction rate also reduced from 3% in year 2020 and 2021 to 1% in year 2022 and year 2023 which caused a rapid fall on the pandemic spread.

    North America

    Mexico was the top most country affected by covid 19 pandemic and closely followed by United states. Mexico population of 128m people constitute to 0.4%Of the total population and 21.3% Of the total North America continents. There is a positive correlation between the total case and the excess mortality. An increase in total case led to an increase in excess mortality. In year 2020, excess mortality grew from 43,943(0.26%) to 213,034(1.25%) in year 2021 and 242,086(1.42%) in year 2022 and greatly decreased to 5,131(0.03%) in year 2023. Reproduction rate was 3% over the first 3 years and there were 1000(One Thousand) Hospital Beds available which might have caused a rapid spread as it can’t curtail the total Mexican population. However, with a fixed GDP of $17336 and 2% of extreme poverty over the 4 years spread, Mexico was able to combat the covid spread by providing Handwashing Facilities, implementing Stringency Index and providing Vaccination for its citizen. Though, the combative measures rate was different and unstable over the years, it helped to reduce the spread. In 2023, there was no combative measures available but the reproduction rate reduced to 1% thereby causing impressive deduction in excess mortality of the country’s population.

    United States of America

    USA population is 338m (56.3%) of North America population, (1.06%) of total population. Excess mortality grew from 24646 (0.15%) in year 2020 to 173056 (1.02%) in year 2022 and drastically reduce to 44964 (0.26%) in year 2023. Though, USA had the highest weekly hospital admission and ICU patient over the 4 years spread, the fixed GDP of 54235 and excess poverty rate of 1% over the 4 years, helped in providing 3000 hospital beds, provided vaccine, boosters and created stringency measures to curb the spread.

    South America

    Ecuador had 18,001,002 population which is the 4.12% of South America population and 0.06% of total population. Excess mortality had a slight increase from year 2020 (0.92%) to year 2021 (1.01%) to year 2022 (1.19%) and reduced in year 2023 (0.69%). With GDP of $10582 and extreme poverty of 4%, Ecuador was able to fight covid 19 spread by providing combative measures like vaccines, boosters and handwashing facilities. Chile had the highest case of covid 19 which accounted for about 17.6% of its total case in South America. This is followed by Brazil which had about 13.2% of its total cases in South America. However, Ecuador had the highest excess mortality followed by Peru. Chile had the highest vaccination and a very high GDP so this helped them reduce the mortality rate.

    Asia

    Israel had the highest case of Covid-19 which accounted to 11.8% followed by Georgia which is about of the total case in Asia. However, Iran had the highest excess mortality 16.1% closely followed by Armenia which is 15.3% of the excess mortality in Asia. Armenia had 3.5% of the male smokers in Asia. Iran had blow GDP of $8788 with of extreme poverty. Armenia had 2% of extreme poverty and GDP of 19083, 0% of stringency rate. There was no precautionary measure in place.

    Oceania

    Guam had the highest total case 0f 15.3% followed by New Zealand which is 13.4% of the Oceania total case. Guam had no excess mortality. Australia had excess mortality followed by New Caledonia. Though, Australia had high GDP, there was no combative measure like handwashing facilities. New Caledonia had hospital bed availableno , had low total vaccination and no GDP.

    Europe

    San Marino had highest total case of 5.92% followed by Austria of 5.48% but had low excess mortality compared to Bulgaria who had the highest excess mortality in Europe. San Marino had 0% of smokers and 0% of extreme poverty. Its GDP was $56861 and had 4000 hospital beds. Australia had relatively high GDP of $45437 and had total vaccination of 11103. Bulgaria had excess mortality of 8.82% followed by Lithuania of 7.14%. Bulgaria had high cardiovascular death rate followed by Lithuania and then, resulted to high excess mortality.
