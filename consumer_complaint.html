Consumer Complaint Analysis

The Consumer Complaint Dataset from the Bureau of Consumer Financial Protection contains records of complaints submitted by consumers about financial products and services. It helps track trends in consumer issues, monitor company responses, and improve financial regulations. Get data here

Brief Overview

The customer complaints dashboard provides a high-level overview of 62,516 total complaints, with 93.8% receiving timely responses. The majority of complaints were submitted via the web, and the top reported issue is "Managing an account." The dashboard includes filters for analyzing trends over time and by product. Additionally, a geographical breakdown highlights complaint distribution by state. Overall, the data suggests a high volume of customer concerns, but efficient response handling.

Key Insights

  1. Total Complaints: The dashboard records 62,516 total complaints, which is a significant number indicating customer dissatisfaction or issues. 93.8% of complaints have been recorded, possibly indicating the percentage of complaints addressed or responded to.
  2. Submission Methods: The bar chart on the left shows the various ways complaints were submitted.The most popular submission method appears to be Web, significantly higher than other methods such as phone, email, or mail.
  3. Top 5 Issues: The middle bar chart highlights the most common customer complaints.The top issue is "Managing an account", followed by other concerns that appear to be related to transactions, fraud, or account access.
  4. Timely Response: The donut chart on the right categorizes responses into timely responses vs. delays. A large portion (93.8%) seems to have received timely responses, which is a positive indicator of efficiency.
  5. Complaints by State: The map highlights the spread of complaints across different states, showing regional variations in customer concerns.
  6. Filter Options: The top-right includes a "Years of Submission" filter (2017 - 2021) to analyze complaints over time. A "Product" filter allows users to focus on specific product-related complaints.

"Managing an Account"

  1. Submission Channels: Web (8,016 complaints) is the most common submission platform. Referral (4,828 complaints) is the second-highest. Phone (1,866 complaints) and other channels (Postal Mail, Fax, and Web Referral) have lower submissions.
  2. Company Response: Majority of complaints (9,048) were closed with an explanation. 5,206 complaints were closed with monetary relief. A small number of cases were closed with non-monetary relief (475) or remain in progress (380).
  3. Timely Response: 94.2% (14,232 complaints) received a timely response. 2.52% (380 complaints) are still awaiting a response.
  4. Geographical Distribution: The map highlights the spread of complaints across different states, showing regional variations in customer concerns.
The high percentage (94.2%) of timely responses suggests that companies are handling most complaints efficiently. However, the dominance of the Web and Referral submission channels indicates a shift towards digital complaint resolutions. The large number of complaints closed with an explanation suggests that many issues may not require monetary compensation but rather better communication or clarification from the company.

"Incorrect Information on Your Report"

  1. Total Complaints: 15,109

  2. Major Submission Channel: Web submissions dominate (4,399 complaints), indicating a preference for digital platforms over phone, referral, or fax.

  3. Company Responses:
    • Closed with Explanation: 3,885 cases, meaning most issues were resolved without direct relief.
    • Closed with Non-Monetary Relief: 770 cases, suggesting companies took corrective actions in some cases.
    • Closed with Monetary Relief: Only 194 cases, meaning financial compensation is rare.
  4. Timely Response: 91.85% of cases received a timely response, but 1.62% (80 cases) are still awaiting action.

"Problem with a Purchase Shown on Your Statement"

  1. Total Complaints: 15,109

  2. Complaint Submission Platform: Web is the most used platform with 3,225 complaints, indicating that most users prefer online submissions. Other platforms like phone (305), postal mail (111), and fax (19) have significantly lower engagement.

  3. Company Response Breakdown:
    • Closed with Explanation: 2,464 cases (most common resolution).
    • Closed with Monetary Relief: 1,811 cases, showing a relatively high percentage of financial settlements compared to other complaint categories.
    • Closed with Non-Monetary Relief: 75 cases. This category represents cases where the company resolved the issue without providing monetary compensation.
    • In Progress: 63 cases (1.43%) remain unresolved. This indicates that a significant number of complaints are still being actively addressed by the companies.
  4. Timely Response Rate: 94.11% of cases received a timely response, which is a strong performance metric. Only 1.43% (63 cases) are still pending.

Recommendations

  1. Consider financial relief for customers who experience financial harm due to incorrect reports.
  2. Introduce real-time tracking so customers can monitor the status of their disputes.
  3. Ensure customer support is available via multiple channels to increase accessibility.
  4. Provide self-help guides on how to dispute errors effectively.
  5. The 1.62% of unresolved cases should be escalated for faster resolution.
  6. Set clear timelines and escalation protocols to prevent delays.
  7. Expand non-monetary relief options such as faster error corrections and improved dispute resolution mechanisms.
  8. Consider financial relief for customers who experience financial harm due to incorrect reports.
